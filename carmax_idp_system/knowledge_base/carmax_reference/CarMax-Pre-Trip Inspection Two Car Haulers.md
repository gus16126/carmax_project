**Step 12:** Air Brake Test 
☐ 1. Verify Wheels Are Chocked.
☐ 2. Parking Brake Valve – Popped Out.
☐ 3. Turn Engine On.
☐ 4. Air Compressor Test.
  ☐ A) Allow Air Pressure Build to Governor Cut-Off (125 psi).
  ☐ B) Turn Engine Off, Ignition On.
  ☐ C) Release Parking Brake (push in).
  ☐ D) Do Not Press Brake Pedal.
  ☐ E) Start a Timer.
  ☐ F) Air Loss for a Single Unit: Less Than 2 psi in 1 Minute.
  ☐ G) Turn Engine On, Let PSI Build to Governor Cut-Off.
  ☐ H) Turn Engine Off, Ignition On.
  ☐ I) Press and Hold Pedal 1 Minute.
  ☐ J) Air Loss for a Single Unit: Less Than 3 psi in 1 Minute.
  Over 3 psi air loss is unacceptable. Check for air leaks before driving.
☐ 5. Low Air Alarm Test.
  ☐ A) Pump Brake Pedal until You Hear the Low Air Alarm (≈55 psi).
  ☐ B) Continue Pumping until You See Red Warning Light Under the Air Tank Gauges.
  ☐ C) Continue Pumping until Brake Air Valve (yellow valve) Pops Out.   

# Air Pressure Buildup Rate 
☐ 1. Turn Engine On.
☐ 2. Start Timer When PSI Reaches 85.
☐ 3. Pressure Builds from 85 to 100 psi in 45 Seconds.
☐ 4. Air Compressor Governor Cut-Off at 125 psi.
☐ 5. Remove Wheel Chocks.   

# Parking Brake Test 
Ensures parking brakes will hold, including during catastrophic air loss.
☐ 1. Parking Brake – On.
☐ 2. Gently Pull Against It.   

# Service Brakes Test 
☐ 1. Release Parking Brake.
☐ 2. Drive Forward (≈5 mph).
☐ 3. Press Brakes.
