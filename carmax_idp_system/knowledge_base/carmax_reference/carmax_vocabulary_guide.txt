# CarMax Vocabulary & Communication Guide
**File:** /docs/CarMax_Vocabulary_Guide.txt  
**Author:** Gustavo Felipe Guallar  
**Created:** 2025-11-03  
**Purpose:**  
To maintain a unified and professional communication tone aligned with CarMax leadership standards across all training, recognition, and documentation messages.  

This guide complements the Control Tower (Instructions Charter) and provides reference phrases, vocabulary tone, and structure consistent with CarMax’s culture and values.

---

## 1. CarMax Communication Philosophy
CarMax managers and associates communicate with a balance of **clarity, respect, and empathy.**  
Every message — whether recognition, instruction, or correction — should:  
- Reinforce teamwork and inclusion.  
- Promote learning and continuous improvement.  
- Reflect accountability without negativity.  
- Inspire confidence and professionalism.  

**Tone:** Calm • Clear • Positive • Constructive  

**Values in Language:**
- **Win Together:** Use inclusive language — *we*, *our team*, *together*, *collaboration*.  
- **Put People First:** Focus on appreciation, support, and recognition of effort.  
- **Go For Greatness:** Encourage improvement and forward momentum.  
- **Do The Right Thing:** Emphasize integrity, accuracy, and ownership.

---

## 2. Vocabulary Standards

| Category | CarMax-Preferred Language | Avoid |
|-----------|---------------------------|--------|
| Appreciation | “Thanks for the support.” / “I appreciate your effort.” | “Good job” (too generic) |
| Teamwork | “We Win Together.” / “Let’s stay aligned as a team.” | “You need to…” (isolating tone) |
| Feedback | “Here’s what went well…” / “Let’s adjust this for next time.” | “You did this wrong.” |
| Improvement | “We can Go for Greatness by improving this step.” | “This needs to be fixed ASAP.” |
| Accountability | “Doing the Right Thing means following this process.” | “Don’t mess this up.” |
| Communication | “Please reach out if you need support.” | “If you don’t understand, ask me.” |
| Safety | “Safety first — take a moment to double-check before moving.” | “Be careful” (vague) |
| Professional Closure | “Thanks again for your attention and teamwork.” | “Let me know” (incomplete close) |

---

## 3. Writing Structure for Managers
Each communication (training, feedback, or recognition) should follow this format:  

1. **Greeting** – Friendly, professional opening.  
   > “Good morning team,” or “Hi [Name],”  
2. **Purpose Statement** – Clarify why you’re writing.  
   > “This message is part of our Delivery Readiness training series.”  
3. **Key Points** – 3–5 bullet points or sentences focusing on clarity and behavior.  
   > “1. Review paperwork before departure.”  
   > “2. Confirm VIN and tag match.”  
4. **Connection to CarMax Values** – Reinforce *why* it matters.  
   > “Doing the Right Thing ensures accuracy and customer trust.”  
5. **Positive Close** – Show appreciation and confidence.  
   > “Thanks for representing our team with professionalism.”

## 5. Message Tone Calibration
When writing, apply this quick tone check:

| Question | Goal |
|-----------|------|
| Is my message clear? | Use direct language without jargon. |
| Is my message respectful? | Reflect understanding, not judgment. |
| Is my message motivating? | Reinforce purpose and pride in the work. |
| Does it align with a CarMax value? | Every message should connect to at least one value. |
