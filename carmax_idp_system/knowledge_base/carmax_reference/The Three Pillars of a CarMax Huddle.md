# AOR = Area of Responsibility.

Once the gap between where you are and where you want to be is identify, the IDP becomes your "living document" to track your progress. At CarMax, these plans often focus on three specific areas of growth:
1. Mastering Your Current AOR
Before moving up, you must show you can own your Area of Responsibility (AOR). This might mean being the "go-to" person for inventory accuracy or becoming the top mentor for new hires in your department.
2. High-Level Competencies
CarMax evaluates potential leaders on specific behaviors. These often include:
* Developing Others: How well you help your peers succeed. ??
* Strategic Thinking: Looking at the "big picture" of the store's performance, not just your own numbers. ??
* Courage: Providing honest, constructive feedback to teammates or even managers. ??

3. "Shoulder-to-Shoulder" Training
Instead of just reading a manual, your IDP will likely include "shoulder-to-shoulder" sessions where you shadow a manager. You might watch how they handle a difficult customer or how they conduct a morning "huddle," then try it yourself under their guidance.

Let's think about that first step-the Career Conversation. If you were preparing for this meeting with your manager tomorrow, which of these do you think would be the most important thing to bring with you?
* A) A list of your top three career goals and why they matter to you.
* B) A list of complaints about your current role.
* C) A request for an immediate pay raise.
What do you think would help your manager support your growth the most?
