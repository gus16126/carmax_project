Gustavo Guallar – Self-Evaluation Summary
LinkedIn Profile: http://www.linkedin.com/in/gustavo-guallar-6b4776118

🟦 Onboarding & Early Contribution – June 2022
After several years as an over-the-road owner-operator, I joined CarMax in June 2022. Shortly after completing my initial training, my then-manager, Jessy Pesantes, selected me to participate in a fleet safety initiative. I traveled to Tampa, FL for a four-day safety training session led by Safety Manager Kyle Morgan.

Upon return, I shared key takeaways with Sr. Fleet Drivers at my store, including practices to improve visibility and safety — such as keeping the upper deck elevated to 8 feet or more, and tucking in lower extensions and ramps when not in use.

🟦 Equipment Safety Improvements – Sept to Nov 2022
Leveraging my experience in light- and medium-duty towing, I identified safety risks with the wheel lifter tie-down and rollback eight-point strapping methods. I consulted with peers in the industry and reviewed expert recommendations. I submitted two detailed safety proposals to my lead driver Luis Rejon, supported by references and procedural guidelines aimed at reducing damage and improving driver safety.

🟦 Rollback Winch Safety Advocacy – Jan to Nov 2023
In early 2023, I raised concerns about the condition and use of winch systems on rollback trucks. I provided photographic evidence of damage and strongly recommended that compromised equipment be taken out of service.

In November 2023, I was invited by management to co-develop a formal winch safety training program. I contributed a draft curriculum based on my experience, police reports, and the Utah Tow Truck Certification Guide. Although the rollback role was phased out shortly after submission, it was rewarding to collaborate on a project focused on proactive fleet safety.

🟦 Role Transition & Operational Innovation – Aug to Sept 2024
Following fleet restructuring, I transitioned to the Home Delivery Team. During this period, I identified operational and logistical challenges in MaxOffer pickups at dealerships like Brickell Mazda and Miami Lakes Auto Mall.

I established direct relationships with dealership contacts, mapped their internal processes, and developed independent workflows that reduced wait times, enabled after-hours pickups, and minimized disruption to their staff. These efforts turned previously challenging accounts into reliable and manageable routes — improving both, delivery efficiency and professional relationships.

🟦 Proactive Maintenance & Structural Safety – August 2024
While inspecting the two-car carrier, I discovered hairline cracks in the welds beneath the upper deck extensions. I analyzed the cause, concluding that pressure from improper loading techniques was the primary factor. To prevent further damage, I implemented a safe-loading adjustment using hydraulic pin settings to create clearance between decks during operation.

This procedure was shared with my team and logistics manager Matthew Douglas to help reduce risk and delay future repairs.

🟦 Peer Training & Knowledge Sharing – Feb 2025
In February, my manager asked me to train a fellow team member, Alex López Guerra, on the CarMax Driver App and MaxOffer delivery process. I provided a step-by-step walkthrough at multiple locations and supported his learning with a custom checklist. Alex quickly demonstrated independence and confidence in completing MaxOffer assignments.

🟦 Equipment Use & Safety Reinforcement – Feb 2025
I raised safety concerns regarding the misuse of the Cottrell QuixSpinz Ratcheting System on our two-car haulers. I noticed improper strap positioning causing unnecessary wear and misalignment, potentially compromising safety.

After confirming correct procedures through Cottrell documentation, I attempted to educate team members and later conducted a hands-on demonstration with Sr. Fleet Drivers and Manager Matthew Douglas. While adoption was limited, I remain committed to improving operational safety through training and communication.

✅ Closing Reflection
Across roles and transitions, I’ve remained committed to identifying risks, proposing actionable solutions, and engaging my peers through collaboration and accountability. Whether addressing equipment safety, streamlining pickups, or supporting team development, I’ve aimed to reinforce CarMax’s values through action — especially Doing the Right Thing, Win Together, and Go for Greatness.

🟦 Peer Training – Ben Gordon

Home Delivery Equipment Training

Training Period: March 17 – April 18, 2025

When I was informed that Ben Gordon would be transitioning into the logistics team, I proactively began drafting a training plan tailored to his learning needs. The objective was to provide a clear, step-by-step introduction to CarMax's home delivery procedures—supported by hands-on guidance that would build his confidence and independence from the very beginning.
This initial plan became the foundation for a repeatable training model, later adapted to support additional trainees joining the team.
Training Focus Areas:
The training plan covered the following key areas essential to Ben’s role as a home delivery driver:

Safe driving behavior and risk awareness

Truck maintenance, cleanliness, and end-of-shift expectations

DOT compliance and regulatory procedures

Pre-trip and post-trip inspections

Vehicle loading, unloading, and alignment safety

Load securement using the Cottrell QuiXspinz system

Use of CarMax mobile tools and apps

MaxOffer delivery and customer interaction protocols

Driving Behavior & Professionalism
Safe Driving Expectations: We reviewed how both on- and off-duty conduct affects driver eligibility and CarMax representation.

Tail Swing Awareness: Ben was coached on rear clearance, mirror use, and turn execution to prevent curbing or collision.

Safe Following Distance: Training emphasized appropriate spacing to accommodate air brake lag and emergency response time.

In-Lane Risk Avoidance: Tactics included maintaining escape paths and avoiding side-by-side travel with other large vehicles.

CarMax Lot Safety: Due to high foot traffic, he was trained to reduce speed, scan continuously, and take extra care near appraisal lanes.

Truck Maintenance & Cleanliness
Daily Cab Cleanliness: Ben was instructed to maintain a clean cab by removing trash and sweeping after each shift.

Glove Disposal: He was trained not to leave rubber gloves in or around the truck after inspections or deliveries.

DOT Compliance & Legal Training
Ben received thorough instruction on the following DOT-mandated responsibilities:

Bill of Lading (BOL): Creating, reviewing, and using both digital and manual BOLs correctly.

ELD Use: My20 ELD setup, inspection logging, screen placement, and Hours of Service compliance.

Timekeeping in UKG: Clocking in only on property and pre-approving time cards for payroll accuracy.

He showed full understanding of these regulatory requirements and demonstrated proper execution in each area.

Tools & App Usage
CarMax Driver App: Ben reached proficiency in navigating the app to locate, verify, and deliver MaxOffer vehicles.

My20 ELD App: He became confident in logging trips, updating statuses, and performing electronic inspections.

Vehicle Handling & Securement
Loading/Unloading Procedures: Ben practiced proper vehicle alignment and single-load transportation of oversized vehicles.

Loading Alignment Correction: He was taught never to adjust vehicles while on the deck; instead, to unload, realign, and reload.

QuiXspinz Tie-Downs: Ben received detailed instruction on the four-point tie-down system, including strap positioning and tension safety.

Hydraulic Safety: Training included pinning the upper deck when not in use and safe step-down lowering methods.

Roadside Loading: He was instructed to work while facing traffic and to minimize exposure when securing roadside vehicles.

Pre-Trip & Post-Trip Inspections
Ben received hands-on instruction daily on identifying and documenting mechanical concerns. He developed consistency in reporting and demonstrated a clear understanding of inspection criteria.

MaxOffer & Customer Delivery Procedures
7110 MaxOffer Delivery: Ben was trained on procedures specific to store 7110, including check-in, parking, and MaxOffer tagging.

Dealership Pickups: He received dealership-specific training for multiple locations and demonstrated strong retention.

Customer Transfers: Ben actively participated in vehicle transfers and home deliveries, practicing CarMax values in direct customer interaction.

Doing the Right Thing
We reviewed how this core CarMax value applies to safety, honesty, and professionalism on the road and in customer-facing moments. Ben aligned with this principle and demonstrated accountability throughout training.

✅ Training Summary & Conclusion
Ben Gordon showed steady improvement in all areas of the training program. He absorbed material quickly, applied feedback effectively, and approached each day with a strong attitude and willingness to learn. His performance consistently reflected CarMax’s values and commitment to safety, professionalism, and teamwork

📅 On August 18, 2025, I shared this reminder to help reinforce one of the most critical parts of our daily operations — the Pre-Trip and Post-Trip Inspections and its standardization.

📌 Why It’s Important to Complete a Pre/Post-Trip Inspection with Each Use
The Pre/Post-Trip Inspection is a critical safety procedure that must be completed every time a vehicle is used. This process has been established to standardize how inspections are performed across the team, ensuring that every driver is aligned with CarMax's expectations for safety, readiness, and operational consistency.

Completing this inspection helps ensure:

Standardization: Everyone follows the same method, reducing errors and building consistency across all shifts and drivers.

Vehicle Safety: Mechanical issues, fluid leaks, or equipment defects can be identified before they cause breakdowns or safety hazards.

Vehicle Readiness: Ensures that the vehicle is properly equipped and prepared for use, reducing downtime and surprises on the road.

Efficiency: Addressing issues before departure prevents delays, rework, and potential incidents during delivery operations.

As a team, it's essential that we not only perform these inspections, but also understand why they matter. This reinforces our commitment to safety, professionalism, and Doing the Right Thing every day.
