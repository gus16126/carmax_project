## 1. Program Overview

### Purpose

To produce a Home Delivery Driver who can independently and safely:
- Complete standardized Pre-Trip and Post-Trip Inspections
- Load, secure, and transport vehicles on a two-car hauler
- Execute MaxOffer pickups and home deliveries
- Comply with all DOT regulations
- Represent CarMax values in every customer interaction

### Program Philosophy

> "We succeed when our people succeed. Integrity is our North Star."

This program follows CarMax's "Grow Your Own" philosophy — developing leaders from within through structured, evidence-based training. Every module ties to a CarMax value and builds toward MAP/IDP readiness.

### Trainee Progression Model

```
WEEK 1: Observation → Guided Practice
WEEK 2: Guided Practice → Independent Execution (supervised)
WEEK 3+: Independent Execution → Sign-Off
```

Each phase gates to the next. Do not advance until the trainee demonstrates proficiency.

### Delivery Method

- **Shoulder-to-Shoulder:** The trainee shadows the trainer, then performs under direct observation
- **Repetition Drills:** Same task repeated 5-10 times for muscle memory (proven with Santiago)
- **Visual Reinforcement:** Labeled diagrams, annotated images, custom checklists
- **Real Examples:** Use actual findings from the field (oil leaks, crack detection, strap wear)
- **CarMax Vocabulary:** All communication uses the CarMax language standards

### MODULE 1: Safe Driving Behavior

**Duration:** 4 hours (2 sessions)
**CarMax Value:** Do The Right Thing
**Location:** CarMax lot + highway

#### Learning Objectives
Trainee will:
- Identify and mitigate tail swing risk
- Calculate and maintain safe following distance
- Execute proper lane positioning with escape paths
- Navigate CarMax lot at safe speeds with continuous scanning

#### Content

**1.1 Tail Swing Awareness**
The rear of a two-car hauler can swing several feet outward during turns. This is a leading cause of curb strikes and side collisions.

*Demonstration:* Have trainee stand at the rear corner while you execute a tight turn. They will see the swing distance firsthand.

*Procedure:*
1. Approach turns wide — use the full lane
2. Check both mirrors before initiating turn
3. Track the trailer's rear corner through the turn
4. If clearance is tight, stop and reassess

**1.2 Safe Following Distance**
Air brakes have a 0.5-second lag before engagement. At 55 mph, the truck travels 40 feet during that lag alone.

*Formula:* 1 second per 10 feet of vehicle length + 1 second. For a two-car hauler (~40 feet): **5 seconds minimum following distance.**

*Practice:* On highway, use a fixed landmark. Count "one-thousand-one, one-thousand-two..." from when the vehicle ahead passes it. You should reach 5 before your truck reaches it.

**1.3 In-Lane Risk Avoidance**
- Always maintain an escape path — never get boxed in
- Avoid traveling side-by-side with other large vehicles
- In multi-lane traffic, position for visibility and options
- When surrounded, gradually adjust speed to create space

**1.4 CarMax Lot Safety**
- Speed: 5 mph maximum in lot
- Continuous scanning for pedestrians, especially near appraisal lanes
- Extra caution during high-traffic hours (mornings, weekends)
- Treat every corner as a potential pedestrian crossing

**1.5 Tight-Space Maneuvering** *(Added from Santiago's training)*
- Practice parking the two-car hauler in confined spaces
- Schedule during low-traffic hours (before 8 AM or after 7 PM)
- Use all mirrors, go slow, stop and reassess if uncertain

#### Trainer Script

> "Safety is the foundation of everything we do. Today we're going to cover the driving behaviors that protect you, our vehicles, and everyone on the road. This isn't just about rules — it's about Doing the Right Thing every time you're behind the wheel. Let's start with something most drivers don't think about until it's too late: tail swing."

#### Practice Activities
1. Lot: Execute 5 tight turns while trainee observes from outside (10 min)
2. Lot: Trainee executes 5 tight turns with feedback (15 min)
3. Highway: Following distance drill with landmark counting (20 min)
4. Lot: Tight-space parking practice — 5 repetitions (15 min)

#### Assessment
- [ ] Trainee can explain tail swing and demonstrate proper turn execution
- [ ] Trainee maintains 5-second following distance on highway
- [ ] Trainee demonstrates continuous scanning in CarMax lot
- [ ] Trainee can park in confined space without assistance

### MODULE 2: DOT Compliance & Documentation

**Duration:** 6 hours (3 sessions)
**CarMax Value:** Do The Right Thing
**Location:** Classroom + truck

#### Learning Objectives
Trainee will:
- Create and verify Bills of Lading (digital and manual)
- Operate My20 ELD for inspections, logging, and HOS
- Clock in/out correctly via UKG
- Understand Hours of Service limits and rest requirements

#### Content

**2.1 Bill of Lading (BOL)**
> *"Never transport a vehicle without proper documentation."*

Every vehicle movement requires a BOL. This is non-negotiable.

*Digital BOL:* Generated through the CarMax Driver App for MaxOffer pickups.
*Manual BOL:* Required for situations without digital options (dealership pickups where app isn't available).

*Manual BOL fields:*
- Date and time of pickup
- Vehicle VIN, make, model, year
- Odometer reading
- Pickup location and destination
- Condition notes (any damage observed)
- Your signature and dealer signature

**2.2 My20 ELD Operation**
- Log into My20 at start of shift
- Select your truck number
- Begin Pre-Trip inspection in My20
- Log all status changes: On Duty, Driving, Off Duty
- Complete Post-Trip inspection in My20 before clocking out

**2.3 UKG Timekeeping**
- Clock in ONLY on CarMax property
- Clock out ONLY on CarMax property
- Approve timecards early in the pay period
- Report any discrepancies immediately

**2.4 Hours of Service (Key Limits)**
- 11 hours maximum driving time
- 14 hours maximum on-duty time
- 30-minute break required after 8 hours
- 10 hours off-duty between shifts

#### Trainer Script

> "Documentation isn't paperwork — it's protection. A proper BOL protects you if a vehicle shows up with damage you didn't cause. Accurate ELD logs protect your CDL. And correct timekeeping... well, that protects your paycheck. Let's walk through each one."

#### Practice Activities
1. Create 3 manual BOLs using real vehicle data (20 min)
2. My20 walkthrough: log in, start inspection, log status changes, end shift (20 min)
3. UKG: clock in, review timecard, approve (10 min)
4. Scenario drill: "Dispatch calls — you need to pick up a vehicle from Brickell Mazda with no digital BOL. What do you do?" (10 min)

#### Assessment
- [ ] Trainee creates a complete, accurate manual BOL from scratch
- [ ] Trainee navigates My20 independently (login, inspection, status changes)
- [ ] Trainee clocks in/out correctly in UKG
- [ ] Trainee can recite HOS limits (11/14/30/10)

### MODULE 3: Pre-Trip & Post-Trip Inspections

**Duration:** 8 hours (4 sessions — this is the longest module for good reason)
**CarMax Value:** Go For Greatness
**Location:** Truck bay

#### Learning Objectives
Trainee will:
- Complete a standardized 500-point Pre-Trip Inspection from memory
- Identify mechanical issues (leaks, wear, damage, loose components)
- Use all inspection tools (gauges, flashlight, checklist)
- Complete a thorough Post-Trip Inspection documenting any issues found

#### Content

This inspection follows the **500-Point Pre-Trip Inspection Checklist** authored by Gustavo Guallar — the CarMax Logistics site standard.

**Step 1: Preparation**
1. Clock into UKG
2. Log into My20 and Start HOS
3. Select your truck number
4. Begin Pre-Trip in My20
5. Verify you have: non-slip shoes, towels, work gloves, thread depth gauge, tire pressure gauge
6. Turn truck's light switch ON
7. Hazard lights ON
8. Remove key from ignition — keep in your pocket
9. Chock rear axle tires (drive wheels) — BOTH sides

**Step 2: Front Cab Inspection** *(14 checkpoints)*
- Upper marker lights, windshield (no cracks), rear view mirrors (condition), wiper blades
- Turning signals, headlights (low/high beam), hood condition, hood hinges, hood latches
- Front grill condition, front bumper (secured)
- No leaks under the truck, suspension (not leaning), front axle condition

**Step 3: Engine Bay — Passenger Side** *(31 checkpoints)*
- Open engine bay. Check: windshield wiper mechanism, coolant reservoir (cap, level, hoses)
- Heater hoses, top radiator hose, radiator (top and side), engine fan, fan clutch
- Radiator shroud, belt condition, alternator (secured, wiring harnesses)
- AC compressor (condition, connectors), belt tensioner, water pump (no leaks)
- Intercooler tube (condition, clamps, boots), engine block, air dryer (hoses, connectors)
- Wipers reservoir (condition, fluid level), air horn (condition/connection)
- Frame rail, shock absorber, suspension hangers, leaf springs, bump stop
- Suspension U-bolt, all castle nuts with cotter pin
- Brake air hose (no leaks), ABS line, brake chamber (secured, no leaks)
- Brake slack adjuster (travel less than 1 inch), brake shoes

*Use labeled engine compartment diagrams as visual reference.*

**Step 4: Engine Bay — Driver Side** *(31 checkpoints)*
Same inspection pattern as passenger side. Check all corresponding components.

**Steps 5–12:** *(400+ additional checkpoints covering:)*
- Cab exterior driver/passenger side
- Bed inspection (all decks)
- Wheel and tire inspection (all positions)
- Air brake test
- Service brake test
- Final walk-around

**Post-Trip Inspection**
- Check for new fluid leaks (oil, coolant, transmission, brake fluid)
- Inspect all straps and tie-downs for wear
- Document any issues found during the shift
- Remove all trash, sweep or air-blow the cab
- Dispose of gloves properly — never leave in truck bed
- Report any maintenance needs immediately

**Real Example — Why This Matters:**
> "During a routine post-trip inspection, I discovered an oil leak from the oil filter area. Had this gone unnoticed until the next shift, it could have led to serious mechanical failure — or worse, a safety incident during delivery. A 2-minute check prevented what could have been a catastrophic failure on the highway." — Gustavo Guallar

#### Trainer Script

> "This is the most important module you'll learn. The Pre-Trip Inspection isn't paperwork — it's your shield. It's what stands between you and a brake failure at 55 mph. We're going to do this inspection together every day until you can do it in your sleep. And even then, we'll keep doing it. Because Doing the Right Thing means never skipping a step, even when no one is watching."

#### Practice Activities
1. Trainer demonstrates full inspection — trainee observes and takes notes (Day 1)
2. Trainee performs inspection with trainer coaching each step (Days 1-3)
3. Trainee performs inspection independently — trainer observes silently (Days 4-5)
4. Repetition drill: Trainee performs inspection 3 times in one session (Day 3 or 4) *(from Santiago's training)*

#### Enhancement: Visual Tools
- Labeled engine compartment diagram (top view, driver side, passenger side, front)
- Wheel inside component diagram
- These are available in the knowledge base and can be printed or shared digitally

#### Assessment
- [ ] Trainee completes full inspection in under 45 minutes
- [ ] Trainee identifies at least 3 planted issues (trainer places simulated issues)
- [ ] Trainee correctly documents all findings in My20
- [ ] Trainee demonstrates proper wheel chock placement
- [ ] Trainee performs post-trip inspection and documents one real finding

### MODULE 4: Vehicle Loading & Unloading

**Duration:** 6 hours (3 sessions)
**CarMax Value:** Win Together
**Location:** Truck bay + CarMax lot

#### Learning Objectives
Trainee will:
- Load and unload vehicles using proper alignment
- Apply single-load protocol for trucks and vans
- Correct alignment errors using the unload/realign/reload method
- Use the controlled two-foot approach for upper deck stop bump

#### Content

**4.1 Loading Alignment**
- Position vehicle centered on the deck
- Use loading guide markers for reference
- Approach slowly — speed causes misalignment
- After positioning: exit vehicle, walk around, verify alignment

**4.2 Single-Load Protocol**
Trucks, vans, and other large vehicles must be transported as single loads — no vehicle on the opposite deck.

**4.3 Alignment Correction**
> *Never adjust a vehicle while it's on the deck.*

If alignment is off:
1. Fully unload the vehicle
2. Realign at the base of the ramp
3. Reload with proper positioning

During Benaiah's training, he initially attempted in-deck corrections. After coaching, he successfully adopted the unload/realign/reload method and followed it consistently.

**4.4 Upper Deck Stop Bump Approach** *(From Benaiah's training)*
Use a controlled two-foot approach:
1. Approach the stop bump slowly with both feet covering the brake
2. Let the vehicle creep forward at idle speed
3. Stop when you feel contact with the bump
4. Do NOT bounce off the bump — this damages equipment and vehicles

**4.5 Hydraulic Safety**
- Always pin the upper deck when not in use
- Use safe step-down lowering — never drop the deck
- Verify deck clearance: use hydraulic pin settings to prevent contact between decks
- This prevents the hairline crack damage discovered on the two-car carrier in August 2024

**4.6 Roadside Loading Safety**
- Face traffic while working on the exposed side
- Maintain situational awareness — minimize phone/distraction
- Position yourself so you can see approaching vehicles

#### Trainer Script

> "Loading looks simple. It's not. Every vehicle you load represents a customer's purchase — sometimes their biggest purchase of the year. Treat it that way. Today we're going to load and unload the same vehicle five times. By the fifth time, your hands will know what to do before your brain has to think about it."

#### Practice Activities
1. Trainer demonstrates: load → verify alignment → unload (15 min)
2. Trainee loads/unloads same vehicle 5 times (from Santiago's training — 1 hour)
3. Upper deck stop bump drill: approach 5 times without bouncing (15 min)
4. Single-load protocol: load a van/truck solo, verify securement (15 min)
5. Roadside loading simulation (in lot): face traffic, maintain awareness (10 min)

#### Assessment
- [ ] Trainee loads a vehicle with correct alignment on first attempt (3 of 3 tries)
- [ ] Trainee demonstrates unload/realign/reload correction method
- [ ] Trainee uses two-foot approach for stop bump without bouncing
- [ ] Trainee pins upper deck when not in use — checked at every break
- [ ] Trainee faces traffic during simulated roadside loading

### MODULE 5: Load Securement — Cottrell QuiXspinz

**Duration:** 4 hours (2 sessions)
**CarMax Value:** Put People First
**Location:** Truck bay

#### Learning Objectives
Trainee will:
- Execute proper four-point tie-down using QuiXspinz 2.0
- Identify correct strap positioning and tension
- Inspect straps for wear, damage, and misalignment
- Reference manufacturer documentation for correct procedures

#### Content

**5.1 Four-Point Tie-Down System**
1. Position straps at all four corners of the vehicle
2. Attach to designated securement points on the deck
3. Ratchet to proper tension — firm but not over-tightened
4. Verify all four points are equally tensioned
5. Double-check after driving 50 feet (re-tension if needed)

**5.2 QuiXspinz Ratchet Alignment**
- Ensure ratchet mechanism is properly aligned — misalignment causes premature wear
- Strap should feed straight into the ratchet, not at an angle
- Inspect strap edges for fraying after each use

**5.3 Strap Inspection**
Check EVERY strap before use:
- [ ] No cuts, frays, or abrasions
- [ ] No chemical contamination (oil, fuel, solvents)
- [ ] Ratchet mechanism operates smoothly
- [ ] No rust or corrosion on metal components
- [ ] Strap is not twisted or knotted

**5.4 Common Errors to Avoid**
- Improper strap positioning causing unnecessary wear and misalignment (observed in field, Feb 2025)
- Over-tightening — damages straps and vehicle suspension
- Under-tightening — vehicle can shift during transport
- Skipping the 50-foot re-check

*Reference: "Safe & Effective Use of Cottrell QuiXspinz 2.0" by Gustavo Guallar (Technical SME Article)*

#### Trainer Script

> "These straps are the only thing between a customer's vehicle and the highway. That's not an exaggeration. Every strap, every ratchet, every check — it's all about the people who trust us with their cars. Let's make sure we earn that trust every single time."

#### Practice Activities
1. Trainer demonstrates full securement on 3 vehicle types — sedan, SUV, truck (15 min)
2. Trainee secures each vehicle type with coaching (30 min)
3. Strap inspection drill: trainer presents 5 straps (some damaged), trainee identifies issues (10 min)
4. 50-foot re-check drill: secure vehicle, drive 50 feet, re-tension (10 min)

#### Assessment
- [ ] Trainee secures a vehicle with all four points at correct tension
- [ ] Trainee identifies 4 of 5 strap defects in inspection drill
- [ ] Trainee performs 50-foot re-check without being prompted
- [ ] Trainee can explain why strap positioning matters (citing real wear example)

4. Complete condition report (note any damage)
5. Scan VIN in Driver App

4. Walk customer through vehicle features

### MODULE 8: Customer Interaction & Professionalism

**Duration:** 2 hours (1 session + ongoing reinforcement)
**CarMax Value:** Put People First
**Location:** Classroom + ride-along

#### Learning Objectives
Trainee will:
- Communicate professionally with customers, dealers, and team members
- Maintain truck cleanliness and professional appearance
- Handle common customer questions and concerns
- Escalate issues appropriately

#### Content

**8.1 Professional Appearance**
- Clean, neat attire — you represent CarMax
- No gloves while driving customer vehicles
- Dispose of rubber gloves after inspections — never leave in truck bed or on floor
- Truck cab: clean at end of every shift — remove trash, sweep, air-blow

**8.2 Customer Communication**
Use CarMax vocabulary standards:

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Here's your car." | "Welcome to your new vehicle. Let me walk you through it." |
| "Sign here." | "I just need your signature to confirm delivery. Take your time." |
| "I don't know." | "That's a great question — let me find the right answer for you." |

**8.3 Common Customer Questions**
- *"Can I return the car if I don't like it?"* → "CarMax offers a 10-day Money Back Guarantee and a 90-day/4,000-mile limited warranty."
- *"What if something goes wrong?"* → "Your vehicle is backed by our Love Your Car Guarantee. If anything comes up, any CarMax store can help."
- *"How do I use this feature?"* → Walk them through it. If you don't know, offer to connect them with a sales consultant.

**8.4 Problem Escalation**
- Customer is unhappy → Listen first. Apologize for the experience. Escalate to supervisor.
- Vehicle has unexpected damage → Document with photos. Report immediately. Do NOT deliver without supervisor approval.
- Dealer conflict → Stay professional. Do not argue. Escalate to logistics coordinator.

**8.5 End-of-Shift Routine**
1. Complete Post-Trip Inspection (Module 3)
2. Remove all trash from cab
3. Sweep or air-blow the cab floor
4. Dispose of all gloves properly
5. Verify all straps and equipment are stowed
6. Log out of My20
7. Clock out in UKG

#### Trainer Script

> "Putting People First isn't a slogan — it's what happens when a customer watches you treat their new car like it's your own. It's the difference between 'here's your car' and 'welcome to your new vehicle.' Every interaction is a chance to deliver the Iconic Experience. Let's make sure you're ready for all of them."

#### Practice Activities
1. Role-play: trainer plays customer with common questions (15 min)
2. Role-play: trainer plays unhappy customer — trainee practices de-escalation (15 min)
3. Truck cleanliness inspection: trainer evaluates cab condition (10 min)
4. Communication style check: trainee rephrases 5 generic statements in CarMax vocabulary (10 min)

#### Assessment
- [ ] Trainee handles 3 common customer questions with correct CarMax responses
- [ ] Trainee demonstrates proper de-escalation technique
- [ ] Truck cab is inspection-ready at end of each shift
- [ ] No gloves found in truck bed or on floor
- [ ] Trainee uses CarMax vocabulary naturally (not forced)

## 5. Trainer Scripts & Prompts

### Daily Opening (Morning Huddle Format)

> "Good morning. Today we're focusing on [module/topic]. Here's why it matters: [value connection]. By the end of today, you should be able to [specific skill]. Let's get started."

*Structure follows the Three Pillars of a CarMax Huddle: Data → Recognition → Lesson.*

### SBI-R Feedback Template

After every practice activity, use this structure:

> **Situation:** "During the loading drill this morning..."
> **Behavior:** "...I noticed you approached the stop bump at a consistent speed and stopped on contact — no bounce."
> **Impact:** "That's exactly the technique that prevents deck damage and keeps vehicles secure. Benaiah mastered this same approach and it's now his standard."
> **Recommendation:** "For next time, try checking your mirror one more time before the final approach — just to confirm the vehicle is centered."

### Daily Debrief
