# New Associate Home Delivery Onboarding Program
## CarMax Logistics — Standardized Training Curriculum

**Author:** Gustavo Guallar, CarMax Logistics
**Based on:** 5 peer training completions (Feb–Jul 2025)
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks (adjustable by trainee pace)
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-24

---

## Overview

This curriculum is built from the proven training model used to onboard Alex Lopez Guerra, Ben Gordon, Benaiah Gordon, Santiago Munarriz, and Carlos Barrios. It follows a **three-phase progression**: Observation → Guided Practice → Independent Execution. Every module ties back to CarMax's four core values.

## Module 1: Safe Driving Behavior (4 hours)

**Objective:** Trainee understands and demonstrates safe driving practices for two-car haulers.

### Topics
- On- and off-duty conduct and its impact on employment
- **Tail Swing Awareness:** Mirror use, turn radius, rear clearance
- **Safe Following Distance:** Air brake lag calculation, highway spacing
- **In-Lane Risk Avoidance:** Escape paths, avoiding side-by-side with large vehicles
- **CarMax Lot Safety:** Low speed, continuous scanning, appraisal lane awareness
- **Tight-Space Maneuvering:** Parking two-car haulers in confined spaces (added from Santiago's training)

### Practice
- Supervised driving in CarMax lot during low-traffic hours
- Highway driving with real-time feedback on following distance and lane positioning

### Value Connection: Do The Right Thing
"Safety isn't optional — it's how we protect ourselves, our team, and our customers."

---

## Module 2: DOT Compliance & Documentation (6 hours)

**Objective:** Trainee demonstrates understanding of all DOT regulatory requirements.

### Topics
- **Bill of Lading (BOL):** Digital and manual creation, review, proper use
- **ELD (My20):** Setup, inspection logging, screen placement, Hours of Service compliance
- **UKG Timekeeping:** Clock-in/out only on CarMax property, early timecard approval
- **Hours of Service:** Legal limits, rest breaks, log accuracy
- DOT regulation overview (49 CFR Parts 391, 392, 395, 396)

### Practice
- Create manual BOLs for simulated MaxOffer pickups
- Complete ELD inspection logs under supervision
- Review real BOL examples from prior deliveries

### Value Connection: Do The Right Thing
"Never transport a vehicle without proper documentation. Compliance protects everyone."

---

## Module 3: Pre-Trip & Post-Trip Inspections (8 hours)

**Objective:** Trainee independently completes a full standardized inspection.

### Topics
- **Why inspections matter:** Standardization, vehicle safety, readiness, efficiency
- Legal requirements and checklist use
- Mechanical issue identification (fluids, belts, tires, brakes, lights)
- Engine compartment components: water pump, belt tensioner, alternator, A/C compressor, drive belt
- Wheel area components: brakes, suspension, tire condition
- **Wheel chock placement** and safety protocols
- Post-trip inspection: identifying leaks, damage, maintenance needs

### Practice
- Daily supervised inspections for the first week
- Independent inspections with review for the second week
- Use of visual aids: labeled engine diagrams, wheel diagrams, inspection checklist
- Real example: oil leak detection and reporting (from Gustavo's own post-trip discovery)

### Tools
- 500-point Pre-Trip Inspection Checklist (Gustavo Guallar, Site Standard)
- Labeled engine compartment diagrams (driver side, passenger side, top view, front view)
- Wheel inside component diagram
- Pre-Trip Assistant reference

### Enhancement (from Santiago's training)
- Repetition drills: load/unload same vehicle multiple times in one session
- Annotated images and diagrams for quick field recall

### Value Connection: Go For Greatness
"A missed inspection step can lead to avoidable hazards. Standardization is how we achieve excellence."

## Module 4: Vehicle Loading & Unloading (6 hours)

**Objective:** Trainee loads and unloads vehicles safely using proper alignment.

### Topics
- **Proper Alignment:** Positioning vehicles on deck, ramp handling
- **Single-Load Protocol:** Trucks and vans transported as single loads
- **Loading Alignment Correction:** Never adjust on deck — fully unload, realign, reload
- **Upper Deck Stop Bump Approach:** Controlled two-foot method (from Benaiah's training)
- **Hydraulic Safety:** Pinning upper deck when not in use, safe step-down lowering
- **Deck Clearance:** Hydraulic pin settings to prevent contact between decks

### Practice
- Repetition drill: Load and unload same vehicle multiple times in 1-2 hour session
- Practice with varying vehicle sizes (sedans, SUVs, trucks, vans)
- Tight-space loading practice

### Value Connection: Win Together
"Proper loading protects the vehicles we transport and the team members who share these trucks."

---

## Module 5: Load Securement — QuiXspinz (4 hours)

**Objective:** Trainee demonstrates correct four-point tie-down using Cottrell QuiXspinz.

### Topics
- **Four-Point Tie-Down System:** Strap positioning, tension safety
- **Ratchet Alignment:** Preventing strap wear and misalignment
- **Strap Inspection:** Identifying wear, damage, improper positioning
- **Cottrell QuiXspinz 2.0:** Full operational use per manufacturer documentation

### Practice
- Hands-on securement of multiple vehicle types
- Inspection of existing straps for wear patterns
- Reference: "Safe & Effective Use of Cottrell QuiXspinz 2.0" (Gustavo Guallar, Technical Article)

### Value Connection: Put People First
"Correct securement isn't just about the vehicle — it's about every driver who shares the road."

### Topics

## Module 7: MaxOffer Pickup & Delivery (6 hours)

**Objective:** Trainee completes MaxOffer pickups and deliveries independently.

### Topics
- **Store 7110 Procedures:** Check-in, parking, MaxOffer tagging
- **Dealership Pickups:** Location-specific protocols, relationship management
- **Multi-Location Releases:** Process for multiple dealership pickups in one route
- **Customer Transfers:** Vehicle handoff, documentation, professionalism

### Practice
- Supervised pickups at 2-3 dealership locations
- Home delivery ride-alongs with real customer interaction
- Independent MaxOffer assignments in final week

### Value Connection: Win Together
"Every pickup and delivery is a reflection of our entire team."

## Module 8: Customer Interaction & Professionalism (2 hours)

**Objective:** Trainee represents CarMax values in every customer interaction.

### Topics
- **Professional Appearance:** No gloves while driving customer vehicles, clean attire
- **Truck Cleanliness:** End-of-shift trash removal, sweeping, air-blowing
- **Glove Disposal:** Proper disposal after inspections — never leave in truck bed
- **Communication:** Clear, courteous, values-aligned
- **Problem Resolution:** Escalation paths, documentation

### Practice
- Ride-along observation during home deliveries
- Role-play: customer questions, delivery issues, dealership interactions

### Value Connection: Put People First
"The customer's experience starts the moment they see our truck."

## Training Phases

### Phase 1: Observation (Days 1–3)
- Trainee shadows trainer on all modules
- Receives visual aids, checklists, and reference materials
- No independent tasks — observation and questions only

### Phase 2: Guided Practice (Days 4–10)
- Trainee performs tasks under direct supervision
- Real-time feedback and correction
- Repetition drills (loading/unloading, inspections, securement)
- Trainer gradually reduces intervention

### Phase 3: Independent Execution (Days 11–14+)
- Trainee performs all tasks independently
- Trainer observes and provides end-of-day feedback
- Final assessment: complete a full-day route with all modules demonstrated
- Sign-off checklist completed by trainer

---

## Training Enhancements (Refined Across 5 Trainees)

1. **Repetition Drills:** Load/unload same vehicle 5-10 times in one session for muscle memory (added for Santiago)
2. **Visual Aids:** Labeled engine diagrams, wheel component images, annotated checklists (developed for Santiago, shared team-wide)
3. **Custom Checklists:** Step-by-step guides tailored to specific dealerships or procedures (used for Alex)
4. **Tight-Space Practice:** Low-traffic CarMax lot sessions for confined maneuvering (added for Santiago)
5. **Real Examples:** Sharing actual inspection findings (oil leaks, crack detection) to show why procedures matter
